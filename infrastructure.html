Overview

As a part of ‘The Big 6’ area for sustainable development in regional manufacturing communities, thus driving America’s economic and national security, infrastructure and site development (I&SD) serves as the backbone and catalyst for growth and development in the manufacturing industry. The overall objective under this pillar is to assess useful regional I&SD information and provide an accessible, user-friendly, and easy-to-comprehend toolkit to analyze it in the context of one or more of the six key manufacturing industries important in that region (defense, energy, transportations, ag, IT, health). Stakeholders could then readily evaluate the current condition of infrastructure development pertaining to the six industries, discerning the current capabilities and gaps. This assessment thus offers clarity and direction to best allocate resources for a holistic, cohesive, and sustainable development in the manufacturing ecosystem.

Methodology

This framework offers a comprehensive and structured approach to help stakeholders narrow down the areas of improvement regarding infrastructure development for targeted manufacturing industry, taking into consideration both the basic requirements and innovation-driven needs to ensure a sustainable development of the manufacturing industry.

Step 1: Identify Satisfying and Enabling Infrastructure for each manufacturing industry

a. Satisfying infrastructure: Infrastructure that meets certain requirements without significantly influencing manufacturers' innovation processes.

Example: volume of vehicle movements or bandwidth.

b. Enabling infrastructure: Infrastructure that provides opportunities for innovation both upstream in the manufacturing of the infrastructure asset and downstream in the supply of new goods and services.

Example: the satellite system.

Step 2: Identify appropriate metrics

These metrics should encompass key dimensions (quantity, quality, safety, etc.) for the target infrastructure type.

Step 3: Gather state data

Utilize governmental database, industry reports, research centers to collect data on every metric identified.

Step 4: Process data

Data should first be normalized through min-max scaling for standardization. Next, users should assign weights for each metric based on its importance in impacting the overall assessment. An overall score for a particular infrastructure type will be calculated based on the standardized data and the weights for each metric within that infrastructure type.

Step 5: Compare with neighboring states

Compare the target state's overall score with neighboring states to identify relative strengths and weaknesses.

Step 6: Identify Infrastructures for Improvement

Prioritize infrastructure that are categorized as ‘satisfying infrastructure’ for specific manufacturing industries.

Step 7: Identify key stakeholders

Engage relevant stakeholders such as government agencies, industry associations, research institutions, and community organizations.

Utah Vesus Neighboring States

Hoping to benchmark Utah with neighboring states as our approach to identify the strengths and weaknesses of Utah in the realm of infrastructure, we calculated an overall score for assessing the current condition of each state’s infrastructure separated by seven infrastructure types. These seven infrastructure types encompass the general categories identified by the satisfying infrastructures for the six manufacturing industries, including transportation, water supply, land, supply of energy, etc. To calculate the overall score, we first normalize the data collected for each metric using min-max scaling, and then place weights for each metric based on the level of importance and relevance.

A Case Study in Utah

While exploring the potential to expand Utah’s solar energy sector, we could follow the methodology to evaluate its current condition of satisfying infrastructure first, which included the supply the electricity, and the supply of energy in this case. We then pick the metrics which are most pertinent to this case study, making sure to include all the dimensions identified (quality, quantity, policy environment, etc.), and list the preliminary findings provided by the dataset. The detailed classification of satisfying infrastructures for each type of industry, the relevant metrics with identified dimensions and the data collected for each metric has been compiled into an Excel document which could be obtained through contacting The American Manufacturing Communities Collaborative (AMCC).

Metrics and Preliminary Findings:

1: Total supply of electricity:

- With 39,386,043 megawatthours in 2022, Utah ranked second to last compared to the neighboring states.

2: Average rental price of electricity:

- With 8.80 cents/kWh in 2022, the average rental price is relatively affordable in Utah compared to neighboring states.

3. Renewable Energy Consumption as a Share of State Total:

- With 68 trillion Btu in 2021, Utah ranked last compared to the neighboring states.

4. Total Renewable Energy Electricity Net Summer Capacity:

- With 2,563.80 megawatts in 2024, Utah ranked last compared to the neighboring states.

5. Number of policies and incentives for renewables and efficiency:

- With 27 existing policies and incentives in 2024, Utah is still lagging neighboring states.

Based on the preliminary findings, we could have a glance of Utah’s current condition of infrastructure necessary for the development of solar energy sector. We could then easily identify the current weaknesses and room of improvement for Utah, thus generating data-supported recommendations like implementing more governmental policies and incentives to help boost the renewable energy capacity and consumption.

Recommendations

Transportation

Governmental agencies like Utah Department of Transportation could promote infrastructure for electrical vehicles, like charging stations, and implement TMS(Transport Management System) to streamline supply chain activites. Research centers like Traffic Lab at the University of Utah and the Mountain Plains Consortium could develop technology to enhance traffic monitoring with Internet of Things(IoT) and artificial intelligence (AI) capabilities.

Energy

The Utah Energy and Power Innovation Center and the Advanced Clean Energy Storage Hub could focus on developing renewable energy installation methods, smart grid technology, and energy storage solutions.

Defense

Governmental agencies like The Utah Department of Veterans and Military Affairs and The Utah National Guard, could collaborate with academia stakeholders like the Space Dynamics Laboratory, as well as the consortium like the Utah Defense Manufacturing Community, to develop innovative technology and defense industrial networks.

IT/Communications

Governmental agencies like Utah Technology Council and Utah's Division of Technology Services could work with the academic and industry leaders in Utah Network for Integrated Computing and Semiconductor Research and Education to develop cloud infrastructure, Internet of Things, 5G technology, enhance cybersecurity, and implement supportive regulatory policies that foster innovation and competition.

Healthcare

The Utah Department of Health and Human Services could provide more funding for the Center for Medical Innovation in the University of Utah, with the goal of develop information technology to leverage data and analytics across the diagnose-treat-monitor ecosystem.

Agriculture

With the support of Utah Department of Agriculture & Food, the Bastian Agriculture Center could participate in START AgriTech to seek public-private partnerships, with the goal of improving farming technology, production systems, and developing climate smart agriculture solutions.

Conclusion

While exploring the current infrastructure condition of a state could be a daunting and massive work, this framework has provided a simple, straightforward, and focused approach which could help local stakeholders take a glance at the selected metrics for evaluating the current infrastructure condition pertaining to the six focused manufacturing industries. It could be easily adjusted to fit any targeted region of interest. At the same time, this database would benefit from more granular research to maximize its utility, and we welcome your collaboration in this endeavor.
