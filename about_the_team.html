Meet Our Team

Yining Lu

Project Manager

Yining Lu is a second-year Public Policy and Management - Data Analytics student at Carnegie Mellon University. She received her bachelor’s degree from Middlebury College majoring in Religion, minor in Psychology and Mathematics. With a thorough liberal arts education, she is deeply driven and motivated by the prospect of building a more connected and nurturing community. She has served as a project manager for the German Chamber of Commerce, and orchestrated a summer camp for high school students in rural China.

Niharika Patil

Technology Lead

Niharika Patil is a second-year Public Policy and Management - Data Analytics track student at Carnegie Mellon University Heinz College. She specializes in applications of data science in the sphere of public policy. Coming from a computer science background, she has three years of experience as a Software Developer at Deloitte Consulting, India. She interned as a Data Science Analyst in the Program Evaluation and Analytics division at the non-profit YWCA. She is also the Secretary of the Women+ in Data Science club at Heinz College, CMU.

Justin Redman

Product Manager

Justin Redman is a second-year Public Policy and Management student at Carnegie Mellon University’s Heinz College. His policy background is in urban planning and economic development and interned at the Regional Industrial Development Corporation of Southwestern Pennsylvania this past summer. He also brings deep experience with data management and geospatial analysis, where he’s managed a relational database and conducted a study on transportation access in growing Loudoun County, VA communities. He also has served as a Head Editor of the Heinz Journal, and brings a broad, diverse skillset to the team.

Vikky Qiu

Communications Lead

Vikky Qiu is a second-year Public Policy and Management student at Carnegie Mellon University. With a profound background in strategic communication consulting and content creation, Vikky assisted various international non-profit organizations with the management of their social media presence in China, including the Gates Venture, the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, the Coalition for Epidemic Preparedness Innovations, etc. Her work focused on the creation and dissemination of engaging narratives and compelling videos, tailored to resonate with the Chinese audience to enhance the visibility, impact, and relevance of these organizations within the dynamic landscape of Chinese social media.

Yi Ren

Finance Manager

Yi Ren is a second-year Public Policy and Management student at Carnegie Mellon University Heinz College. Before attending graduate school, Yi Ren interned at Deloitte China, where she provided audit services and collected fund information for Yinhua Fund Management project. She also interned at AISTAR Venture Capitalist, where she analyzed company-wide and industry-wide data extracted from Wind that estimates Tesla and other new energy vehicles and presented findings through reports.

Eli Ben-Michael

Project Faculty Advisor

Eli Ben-Michael is an assistant professor in the Department of Statistics & Data Science and the Heinz College of Information Systems and Public Policy at Carnegie Mellon University. Previously, he was a postdoctoral fellow in the Institute for Quantitative Social Science and the Department of Statistics at Harvard University. He received his PhD in Statistics from U.C. Berkeley and spent his undergraduate years at Columbia University where he received a bachelors degree in computer science and statistics. His research focuses on developing statistical and computational methods to solve practical issues in public policy and social science research. He is particularly interested in bringing together ideas from statistics, optimization, and machine learning to create methods for credible and robust causal inference and data-driven decision making.
